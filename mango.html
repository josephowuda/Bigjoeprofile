What is Mango?

The mango comes from the tree Mangifera indica. It is often called the “king of fruits” because of its rich flavor, bright color, and high nutritional value. it is a sweet, juicy tropical fruit that is widely loved around the world, especially in warm regions like Africa and Asia.

Description

    Shape: Oval or slightly round
    Skin color: Green, yellow, orange, or red (depending on the variety)
    Flesh: Soft, juicy, and usually golden-yellow
    Taste: Sweet, sometimes slightly tangy

Where Mango Grows?

Mangoes grow best in tropical and subtropical climates. Countries like India, Nigeria, and Thailand produce large quantities of mangoes.

Nutritional Benefits

Mangoes are very healthy. They contain:

    Vitamin C (boosts immunity)
    Vitamin A (good for eyes and skin)
    Fiber (helps digestion)
    Antioxidants (protect the body from diseases)

Uses of Mango

    Eaten fresh as a fruit
    Made into juice, smoothies, and desserts
    Used in cooking (e.g., sauces, salads, and stews)
    Dried or processed into snacks
